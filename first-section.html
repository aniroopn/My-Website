Research(Bioinformatics)

One of the significant challenges in genomics is understanding the functions of all genes. Proteins are the translations of the DNA gene sequences, the cells' main actors. Our research uses a computational approach to assign protein functions that rely on identifying close relatives. It delves into bioinformatics; we apply an AI method that Dr. Jernigan and his team created to annotate Proteins and Genes. We can select different viruses based on their genome size and gain comprehensive know-how of the functionalities. A real-world application would be using workflow for investigating the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). Our homology search uses three main tools: BLAST, PROST, and Foldseek. They each have their uses within sequence or structural comparison. As for our validation process, we use FATCAT for flexible structural alignment and AlphaFold2 for predicting protein structures. Now, we also need a platform to showcase our results. We use JSONWP, which helps us transform the bioinformatics results into an interactive web page format. Our research takes the homologies found for each protein of a viral genome and compares them. This comparison is made between the different methods that find the homologies. We hope to find new insights into the viral genome and increase our knowledge about their effects on the host. Our research is fundamental because understanding viral proteins is the key to fixing the implications caused by them.
